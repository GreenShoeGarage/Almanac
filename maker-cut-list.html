MAKER CUT LIST
A two-dimensional layout planner for sheet goods. Pack parts onto stock with minimal waste, handle multiple sheets, account for blade kerf, and produce a printable cut sheet.

EDITION I · VOL. 1
StockFIG. 1
Standard plywood is 48 × 96 inches (4 × 8 ft). Kerf is the material removed by the cut; 1/8" (0.125") for a typical table-saw blade, 0.1 mm for a laser. Available is how many sheets you have on hand; the optimizer will use as many as needed up to this number.
Parts to CutFIG. 2
Name W H Qty Rotate
"Rotate" lets the part be placed in either orientation. Turn off if the part has a grain or a directional pattern that must stay aligned with the sheet.
OptionsFIG. 3
"Best fit" places each part where it creates the least new waste. "Guillotine shelf" packs in horizontal rows for easy table-saw cuts. Sort affects which parts are placed first; largest-first usually packs most efficiently.
